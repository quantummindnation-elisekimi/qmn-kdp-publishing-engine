# Copyright and IP Policy — QMN KDP Publishing Engine

*This document governs all book production in this repo. Every phase must be checked against these rules.*

---

## Why This Matters

Publishing a book that infringes on copyright or trademark exposes QMN to takedown requests, legal liability, damaged reputation, and Amazon account suspension. These risks are real and preventable with proper process.

---

## Copyright Basics for Children's Books

Copyright protects:
- Written text (story, dialogue, verse)
- Illustrations and visual art
- Character designs when documented as original creative works
- The specific combination of story elements in a protected work

Copyright does **not** protect:
- Ideas, themes, or concepts (e.g., "a child is scared of the dark" is not copyrightable)
- Names alone (though names can be trademarked)
- Common words and phrases
- Historical facts

---

## Trademark Basics

Trademarks protect brand names and logos used in commerce. In children's publishing this includes:
- Character names used across multiple products (e.g., Peppa Pig, Bluey, Clifford)
- Series names registered as trademarks
- Publisher imprint names and logos
- School/organization names used as brands

## Forbidden Content — No Exceptions

### Characters and Brands
- No Disney characters: Mickey Mouse, Elsa, Moana, Simba, Buzz Lightyear, and all others
- No Pixar characters
- No DreamWorks characters: Shrek, Puss in Boots, and all others
- No Nickelodeon characters: SpongeBob, Dora, Blue (Blues Clues), Rugrats, and all others
- No PBS characters: Daniel Tiger, Curious George, Arthur, and all others
- No Netflix/Amazon/streaming original characters
- No Sesame Street characters: Elmo, Big Bird, Cookie Monster, and all others
- No Dr. Seuss characters: The Cat in the Hat, The Grinch, Lorax, and all others
- No Eric Carle characters: The Very Hungry Caterpillar
- No Peppa Pig, Bluey, Ben & Holly, or any current animated series characters
- No classic literary characters that may still be trademarked (Paddington Bear, Madeline, etc.)

### Artist Style References
- No illustration style references to living artists
- No illustration style references to recently deceased artists whose estates may still hold rights
- No phrases like "in the style of," "similar to," "inspired by [named illustrator]"
- Original visual concept descriptions only in all illustration prompts

### Brands and Celebrities
- No real company names, logos, or products inside the story
- No celebrity names, likenesses, or quotes
- No famous place names used in ways that could imply endorsement
- No school, organization, or charity names without explicit written permission

### Concept Copying
- Do not copy the premise, plot structure, or character arc of any identifiable competing book
- Do not use the same title or a title that is confusingly similar to an existing book
- Do not reproduce any text from another published work, even short phrases if they are iconic

## Required Checks Before Finalizing

### Character Name Check
1. Search the character name on the USPTO trademark database (uspto.gov/trademarks)
2. Search the character name on Amazon Books (free text search)
3. Search the character name on Google with "children's book character"
4. If any results return a known trademarked children's character with that name — change the name
5. Document the search date and result in `09_copyright_check.md`

### Title Check
1. Search the exact proposed title on Amazon Books
2. Search the exact proposed title on Google Books
3. If an existing title matches exactly — revise the title before publishing
4. Document the search date and result in `09_copyright_check.md`

### Illustration Concept Check
1. Review every illustration prompt for any style references — remove all artist names
2. Review character visual descriptions against known character databases if uncertain
3. Document the review date in `09_copyright_check.md`

### AI-Generated Content Check
1. If any AI image generator was used for illustrations, review the generator's terms of service for commercial publishing rights
2. Document the generator used and confirm commercial use is permitted
3. Complete Amazon KDP's AI content disclosure (see `docs/ai_disclosure_policy.md`)

---

## Must-Do Documentation

For every book, `09_copyright_check.md` must be completed and signed by Elise before any KDP submission. This file must include:
- Character name search results and dates
- Title search results and dates
- Illustration concept review confirmation
- AI disclosure status
- Canva asset license confirmation
- Elise's sign-off

---

## Human Review Requirement

No copyright check can be completed by AI alone. Elise must personally review the copyright check document and sign off. AI can draft the checklist and flag items for review, but final sign-off is always human.

---

## What Happens If a Risk Is Found

If any of the above checks reveals a potential copyright or trademark issue:
1. Stop work on the affected element immediately
2. Flag the issue to Elise with a clear explanation
3. Propose an alternative (new name, revised concept, etc.) before proceeding
4. Do not publish until the risk is resolved and documented

---

*This policy applies to all books in this repo. It is not optional. When in doubt, stop and check.*
