# Amazon Listing Rules — QMN KDP Publishing Engine

*Guidelines for writing KDP listings that serve buyers, comply with Amazon policy, and optimize for discovery.*

---

## The Listing's Job

The KDP listing has two jobs:
1. Help the right parent find this book through search
2. Convert that parent into a buyer

Everything in this guide serves one of those two jobs.

---

## Title Rules

**What works:**
- Lead with the emotional experience or transformation the book delivers
- Keep it short, memorable, and thumbnail-readable (the cover title and the listing title should match)
- Test the title: would a parent recognize what this book is about from the title alone?

**What doesn't work:**
- Keyword-stuffed titles ("Children's Book About Trying New Things For Kids Ages 4-7 Preschool Growth Mindset")
- Vague titles ("The Little Book" or "A Story About Being Brave")
- Titles that exactly match existing Amazon titles

**KDP Policy:** Amazon can suppress listings for keyword-stuffed titles. Keep it clean.

**Before finalizing:** Search the exact proposed title on Amazon Books. If an existing title matches exactly — change it.

---

## Subtitle Rules

The subtitle carries search keywords. Write it for the parent's search intent.

**Effective format:** "[What the book does] for [specific audience]"

Examples:
- "A Storybook for Kids Who Feel Scared to Try New Things"
- "Helping Children Ages 4–7 Build Emotional Courage"
- "A Picture Book About Overcoming Fear and Finding Bravery"

**Include:** 1–2 naturally placed keyword phrases relevant to what parents search
**Avoid:** Stuffing 5+ keywords in a row; subtitle should read naturally aloud

**Length:** Keep under 200 characters.

---

## Book Description Structure

Use this structure consistently:

```
[HOOK — 1-2 sentences]
Speak directly to the parent's pain point. Use language from real reviews.
Example: "Does your child freeze up when faced with something new? 
You've seen it — the tears before swimming lessons, the meltdown before 
the first day of school."

[STORY SUMMARY — 2-3 sentences]
What happens in the book. What the child learns.

[VALUE STATEMENT — 1-2 sentences]
What the parent and child will experience together. 
Focus on the emotional outcome, not the plot.

[SERIES/BRAND NOTE — 1 sentence]
Position within QMN/MindQuest series if applicable.

[CALL TO ACTION — 1 sentence]
"Scroll up and grab your copy today."
```

**Formatting tips:**
- Use line breaks between sections for readability
- Bold the hook line if the KDP system allows formatting
- Keep the total description under 400 words
- Do not use ALL CAPS excessively
- Do not make false claims: "the #1 book for anxious kids" (unless verified)
- Do not guarantee outcomes: "this book will cure your child's fear" is not allowed

## Backend Keywords

Amazon KDP allows 7 keyword fields. Each field accepts a phrase up to 50 characters.

**How to choose backend keywords:**
1. Start with phrases parents actually type in Amazon search (not what you want to rank for)
2. Use longer-tail phrases — they're more specific and easier to rank for
3. Do not repeat words from your title and subtitle (Amazon already indexes those)
4. Do not include author names, competitor names, or Amazon-restricted terms
5. Do not use words like "bestselling," "free," or "#1"
6. Verify search demand manually by typing phrases in Amazon search and observing the autocomplete and result count

**Sample keyword framework for children's emotional books:**
- [emotion] books for kids ages [range]
- picture books about [specific theme]
- [age group] storybooks about [topic]
- children's books [specific situation] (e.g., "starting school," "new baby sibling")
- [benefit]-focused children's books
- growth mindset picture books [age range]
- read aloud books for [age/grade]

**Validate** all keywords against current Amazon search demand at time of submission. Keyword effectiveness changes.

## Category Selection

Select 2 categories. Strategy:

**Category 1:** Choose a broad, high-traffic category that accurately describes the book
- Example: Children's Books > Growing Up & Facts of Life > Emotions & Feelings

**Category 2:** Choose a more specific category where the competition BSR is reachable
- Look at the top 5 books in the specific category and note their BSRs
- If the top book in the specific category has a BSR of #50,000 in books, getting into that category's top 10 is much more achievable than a category where the top book is #500

**After publishing:** Categories can be updated. If a better fit is identified, request a category change through KDP support.

---

## Age Range and Reading Level

- Set the age range to the **actual target audience** — do not expand it dishonestly to reach more searches
- Set the US grade level range to match (Pre-K through 1st for ages 4–7)
- Accurate age ranges help Amazon serve the book to the right buyers

---

## Pricing Notes

**Print pricing:**
- KDP picture books typically sell for $7.99–$13.99
- Do not price below KDP's minimum (which is based on page count and printing cost)
- Consider the perceived value — a lower price can signal low quality to buyers
- $9.99–$12.99 is a common sweet spot for independently published 32-page picture books

**eBook pricing:**
- If publishing an eBook version, $2.99–$4.99 is typical for children's picture books
- KDP Select (Kindle Unlimited) requires eBook exclusivity — evaluate whether that tradeoff is worthwhile

**Launch pricing:**
- A temporary lower price during launch week can help initial velocity
- Only do this if KDP Select terms allow, and only for a defined limited period
- Do not permanently underprice a book that has genuine value

## A+ Content (If Eligible)

KDP allows A+ Content (enhanced listing pages) for enrolled books. If eligible:
- Use A+ Content to show interior pages
- Use A+ Content to introduce the character
- Use A+ Content to show the activity pages
- A+ Content significantly improves conversion — prioritize it when available

---

## Post-Launch Optimization

- Review KDP analytics 30 days after launch
- Update backend keywords based on what search terms are driving impressions
- Test a description revision if conversion is low
- All metadata changes require Elise review before updating the live listing
- Do not make changes based on one week of data — give the listing time to stabilize

**No guaranteed outcomes.** All optimization is iterative. Amazon rankings fluctuate. Real results require patience and data.
