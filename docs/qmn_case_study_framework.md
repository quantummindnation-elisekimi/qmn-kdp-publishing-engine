# QMN Case Study Framework — QMN KDP Publishing Engine

*How to document each book project as a transparent, useful QMN/MindQuest case study.*

---

## Purpose of Case Studies

QMN case studies serve two audiences:

1. **Internal:** Document what worked, what didn't, and what to do differently on the next book. Build a real knowledge base from real publishing data.

2. **External (eventually):** Demonstrate QMN's publishing system to potential partners, clients, or community members. Show the real process — research, production, results — with honest numbers.

These case studies are **not marketing copy**. They are honest operational documentation. Do not inflate results. Do not omit failures.

---

## When to Start and Complete a Case Study

- **Start:** After the book is submitted to KDP (create the file; populate sections 1–5)
- **First update:** 30 days post-publication (populate sections 6–7 with initial data)
- **Second update:** 90 days post-publication (populate sections 8–9 with fuller picture)
- **Final version:** After Elise reviews and approves for any external use

---

## Case Study Structure

### Section 1: Project Overview

- Book title and subtitle
- Target audience (age range, theme)
- Date research started
- Date submitted to KDP
- Date published (if approved)
- Status: Published | Under Review | Rejected (with reason)

### Section 2: Research and Niche Selection

- Which niche was selected and why
- Niche score summary (total score, key strengths and weaknesses)
- What competitors were observed
- What the review mining revealed
- What gap the book was designed to fill
- How long research took

### Section 3: Production Process

- How long each phase took (research, writing, character development, illustration, design, metadata)
- What tools were used (Claude, Canva, image tools, etc.)
- What AI did vs. what Elise directed/edited
- Any phases that needed to be redone and why
- Approximate total production time

### Section 4: Copyright and Policy

- Copyright checks completed (summary — no sensitive details)
- Any IP risks identified and how they were resolved
- AI disclosure status
- Canva asset documentation status

### Section 5: KDP Submission

- What categories were selected and why
- Backend keywords used
- Initial pricing
- Any KDP review flags or issues during submission
- Time from submission to publication (KDP review typically 24–72 hours)

### Section 6: First 30 Days

- Sales (units — be honest; zero is a real result)
- KDP page reads (if in KDP Select)
- BSR observed (screenshot or note)
- Any review activity
- What was changed in the listing post-publication and why

### Section 7: Post-Launch Adjustments

- Any keyword changes made
- Any description revisions
- Any price changes
- Any category changes
- What prompted each change

### Section 8: 90-Day Assessment

- Cumulative sales (units)
- Revenue (optional — Elise's discretion)
- Current BSR trend
- Review count and average rating
- What worked in the listing
- What did not work

### Section 9: Lessons Learned

- What would be done differently on the next book
- What surprised Elise about the process
- What the data confirmed or contradicted about the original niche hypothesis
- Recommendations for future books in this niche or series

### Section 10: QMN Brand Impact

- Did this book reinforce QMN/MindQuest brand positioning?
- Any feedback from readers or parents
- Any community or social response
- Potential for series continuation, activity book companion, or other extensions

---

## What Case Studies Must Not Claim

- Do not claim guaranteed results from the process
- Do not imply that following these steps will produce the same sales outcomes
- Do not misrepresent production costs, time, or effort
- Do not present AI-generated content as entirely human-written

---

## Publication of Case Studies

Case studies are internal documents by default. To publish a case study externally:
- Elise must review and approve the full document
- Any revenue figures included must be accurate
- Any identifiable third-party information must be reviewed for appropriateness
- The case study must clearly identify AI's role and human editorial oversight
