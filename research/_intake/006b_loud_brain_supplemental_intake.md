## Review mining  (your own words — never paste review text)

praise: When My Brain Is Messy reviews are almost entirely from parents of autistic or neurodivergent children. Reviewers praise the book for giving their child a relatable character (autistic dinosaur) who experiences sensory challenges similar to theirs. Multiple reviewers note that their children now use the book's language ("my brain feels messy") to describe their state, which creates a communication bridge. Psychologists and OTs recommend it for children who struggle to articulate their internal experience. Reviews also come from siblings, cousins, and teachers seeking to understand neurodivergent children in their lives.

wishes: Reviews are tightly focused on autism/neurodivergence — parents wish the book had more exercises or a companion guide. No reviews from parents of neurotypical overwhelmed children (not the audience for this book).

problem_language: "autistic," "neurodivergent," "sensory differences," "loud noises and bright lights," "deep pressure," "overstimulation," "overwhelmed," "my brain feels messy." The "loud brain" language that parents are searching for is DISTINCT from the "messy brain" language in this competitor — there is no children's book that uses the specific "loud brain" / "getting louder" framing for general overwhelm.

unmet_needs: The "My Brain Gets Really Loud" search phrase returns books specifically for neurodivergent/autistic children with sensory processing differences. A QMN book positioned for the GENERAL overwhelm experience (all children before a meltdown) would occupy a separate, unoccupied positioning — not competing directly with autism-specific titles. This is a differentiation advantage, not a liability.
