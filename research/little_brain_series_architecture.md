## Why This Document Exists

Gate 7 niche research identified that the "Little Brain" naming convention used in Book 001 (*The Little Brave Brain*) has strong series potential. If Elise validates adjacent niches 006–008 on Amazon, multiple books could share a series identity, enabling:

- Amazon "customers also bought" cross-sells between books
- Series-based search visibility (parent searching one book finds others)
- Consistent visual brand across covers
- Compounding reviews and author authority over time

**This document exists to plan the series architecture before any book is produced — so the series makes sense as a whole, not just book by book.**

## Series Brand Identity

**Series name:** The Little Brain Series (working title)
**Publisher brand:** MindQuest / QMN
**Core premise:** Each book gives a child's inner emotional experience a name — not a diagnosis, not a behavior label, but a brain-based story that makes the child feel understood rather than fixed.
**Tone:** Warm, intelligent, honest. Not preachy. Not clinical. Re-readable.
**Age range:** 4–7 years (consistent across all books)
**Format:** Storybook — not a workbook, not an activity book

## Series Rules (Must Follow Across All Books)

These rules apply to every book in the series. No exceptions without Elise approval.

### Character Rules
- All brain characters are original — not resembling Inside Out, Pixar, Disney, or any franchise character
- Character names must be searched on USPTO.gov before any book enters production
- Visual design of brain characters must not use a control-room motif, emotion-color coding system, or any concept that matches Inside Out's registered visual world
- Characters should feel like individuals — not generic emotion icons

### Story Rules
- Each book is a standalone story — does not require reading other books in the series to be understood
- Each book follows a real emotional experience from a child's point of view — not a lecture, not a lesson plan
- Each book ends with the child feeling understood and slightly more capable — not fixed, not lectured
- No moralizing. No worksheets embedded in the narrative.

### Visual Rules (for illustration brief stage — not now)
- Consistent visual branding across covers: same title treatment, same series identifier
- Cover art style must be original — no artist style references allowed
- All illustration prompts must describe original visual concepts only

### IP Rules
- No famous character, brand, mascot, or licensed property referenced visually or textually in any book
- All character names searched for trademark risk before finalizing
- See `docs/copyright_policy.md` for full rules

## Series Naming Convention

**Working title format:** The Little [Adjective] Brain
- The Little **Brave** Brain (Book 001)
- The Little **Loud** Brain (Book 002)
- The Little **Calm** Brain (Book 003)

**Why this works:**
- "Little" signals age-appropriate and non-threatening
- "Brain" ties to MindQuest brand identity (the brain as the hero, not the problem)
- The adjective changes per book — varies the entry point while keeping the series cohesive

**Risk to validate:** Confirm no existing "Little [Adjective] Brain" series exists on Amazon or as a trademark before committing to the naming convention.
