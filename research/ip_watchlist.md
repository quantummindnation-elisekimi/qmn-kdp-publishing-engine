## IP Risk Entries

### 1. Inside Out (Disney/Pixar)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Status** | Open |
| **Risk level** | Medium |
| **IP owner** | Disney / Pixar |
| **What's protected** | Animated film franchise (2015, 2024) featuring anthropomorphized emotions as characters operating a "brain control room" inside a child's head. Color-coded emotion palette (Joy = yellow, Sadness = blue, Anger = red, etc.). Bing Bong, the memory dump concept, specific character designs. |
| **Relevant niches** | 004 (Anger Management), 006 (Loud Brain / Overwhelm), 008 (Finding Calm) — any niche involving brain or emotion characters |
| **Risk scenario** | A QMN book that personifies emotions as characters inside a child's head, depicts a "headquarters" or control room, or uses Inside Out's emotion color-coding could create consumer confusion or legal exposure. |
| **How to steer clear** | Child is the protagonist — not personified emotion characters. Brain is something the child experiences (a sensation, a state), not a setting where characters live. No "control room" or "headquarters" visual metaphor. No emotion color-coding that mirrors Inside Out's palette. Document these guardrails in every illustration brief before art direction begins. |
| **Date added** | 2026-06-19 |
| **Next action** | Document art direction guardrails before any illustration brief is written for 004, 006, or 008 |

### 2. "Brave Brain" Title Collision

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Status** | Open |
| **Risk level** | Medium-High |
| **IP owner** | Erin Reese Johnson (self-published, Nov 2025) |
| **What's protected** | "The Story of Little Heart and Brave Brain: Understanding Feelings and Finding Calm" — a self-published children's storybook using characters named "Little Heart, Brave Brain, and Body." 29 pages, ages 4–9. Currently 2 reviews (negligible traction). Miscategorized under adult Self-Help. |
| **Relevant niches** | 006 (Loud Brain — Book 002 in Little Brain series), 008 (Finding Calm — Book 003); and directly QMN Book 001 "The Little Brave Brain" |
| **Risk scenario** | "Brave Brain" as a character name now exists in print. QMN Book 001 is "The Little Brave Brain." If QMN Book 001 gains traction before a USPTO trademark is filed, this existing publication could complicate trademark registration or create consumer confusion. |
| **How to steer clear** | (1) Confirm QMN has USPTO trademark application underway or planned for "The Little Brave Brain" title. (2) Monitor this competitor — weekly BSR check for first 6 months after QMN Book 001 launches. (3) Document that QMN's Little Brain character design is a single child protagonist experiencing brain states — conceptually distinct from Little Heart and Brave Brain's cast of body-part characters. |
| **Date added** | 2026-06-19 |
| **Next action** | Elise to initiate USPTO trademark search on "The Little Brave Brain" before Book 001 goes to production |

### 3. Dragon Metaphor in Anger Context

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Status** | Monitoring |
| **Risk level** | Avoidable — do not use |
| **IP owner** | Steve Herman / Dragon Master Publishing ("Train Your Angry Dragon" series, ~5,000 reviews) |
| **What's protected** | The dragon character in an explicit anger-management context, the "train your angry dragon" framing, the series brand. |
| **Relevant niches** | 004 (Anger Management broadly) |
| **Risk scenario** | Any QMN character using a dragon in an anger or big feelings context would be directly associated with the dominant competitor in the niche and could create IP/trademark confusion. |
| **How to steer clear** | Do not use a dragon metaphor, dragon character, or "tame the angry [creature]" framing in any QMN book. QMN's brain-first approach avoids animal metaphors entirely. |
| **Date added** | 2026-06-19 |
| **Next action** | No action required — standard guardrail. Flag in any illustration brief that requests an animal-metaphor character. |

### 4. "Spot" / Blob Metaphor and "A Little ___" Naming Pattern

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Status** | Monitoring |
| **Risk level** | Avoidable — do not use |
| **IP owner** | Diane Alber ("A Little SPOT" series, 56-book series, 3,485+ reviews) |
| **What's protected** | The "SPOT" character, the spot/blob visual metaphor for emotions, the "A Little SPOT of ___" title pattern, and the associated series brand. |
| **Relevant niches** | 004 (Anger Management), any niche using blob or spot characters to represent emotions |
| **Risk scenario** | Any QMN book using a geometric blob or spot as an emotion character, or naming pattern "A Little ___ of ___," risks confusion with Diane Alber's extensive and well-known series. |
| **How to steer clear** | Do not use spot, blob, or geometric-shape characters to personify emotions. Do not use the naming pattern "A Little SPOT of ___" or any close variation. QMN's "The Little ___ Brain" naming pattern is distinct — it uses "brain state" not "emotion spot." |
| **Date added** | 2026-06-19 |
| **Next action** | No action required — standard guardrail. Confirm QMN's "The Little ___ Brain" series title is differentiated enough (it is — brain state vs. emotion spot are categorically different). |
