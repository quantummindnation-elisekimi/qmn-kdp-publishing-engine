## Market Gap Hypothesis

*Pre-filled for Elise to validate or reject through research.*

**Hypothesis:** Most bedtime fear books use one of two approaches:
1. Monster-reassurance ("the monster is actually friendly")
2. Parent presence ("you're not alone / I'll protect you")

A QMN angle would be different: focusing on *why the brain gets activated at bedtime* and teaching the child to understand and gently calm their own brain, rather than needing external reassurance. This positions the child as capable of self-soothing — a more empowering message.

**Validation required:** Do reviews mention children becoming more independent sleepers after reading a book? Do parents wish their child could self-soothe instead of needing them to stay?
