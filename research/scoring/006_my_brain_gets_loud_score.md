## Market Gap Hypothesis

*Pre-filled for Elise to validate or reject through research.*

**Gap identified:** Most children's books about anger or big feelings start at the emotional label ("I feel angry"). This niche starts earlier — at the moment of overwhelm, when the brain gets louder than the child can manage. No existing storybook appears to center this pre-anger, sensory-flooding moment as its core story problem.

Additionally, "loud brain" is informal parent language already in wide use (search parenting forums, OT community language) — which may indicate real buyer search intent that hasn't yet been met by a picture book.

**Validation required:** Does Elise find any book that already owns the "loud brain" or "overwhelm before anger" framing in storybook form? If not — gap is real. If yes — assess whether QMN can differentiate on age range, tone, or series positioning.

## Copyright / IP Risk Notes

*Preliminary — Elise confirms during research.*

- "Loud brain" as a phrase — confirm no existing trademark or registered character name before finalizing
- **Inside Out (Pixar/Disney) proximity is the primary risk** — do not use anthropomorphized brain characters, emotion characters living inside a head, or control-room imagery
- Do not use any brain character that is round, colorful, and expressive in a way that resembles Inside Out's visual world
- "Brain" as a concept is unprotected — what is protected is Pixar's specific character designs and Inside Out's registered story elements
- Validate character name (if any brain character is used) on USPTO.gov before finalizing
