## Market Gap Hypothesis

*Pre-filled for Elise to validate or reject through research.*

**Hypothesis:** Most books in the friendship niche focus on how to *be* a good friend — sharing, kindness, including others. These are important but address the socially confident child. The underserved angle is the child who *wants* a friend but doesn't know how to start — the one who stands at the edge of the group, watching, too scared to approach.

A QMN book in this niche would focus on the internal experience of social fear: the worry that no one will like you, that you'll say the wrong thing, that you'll be rejected. And it would show one small, brave step — the moment of reaching out — and what happens when you do.

This is a different emotional positioning from "be kind and include others" and from "here are 5 tips for making friends."

**Validation required:** Do reviews for existing friendship books mention that the child *who already struggles socially* didn't connect with the book? Do parents say the book was "too cheerful" or "didn't address the fear"?
