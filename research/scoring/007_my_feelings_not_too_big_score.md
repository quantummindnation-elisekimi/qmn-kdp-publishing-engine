## Market Gap Hypothesis

*Pre-filled for Elise to validate or reject through research.*

**Gap identified:** The majority of children's emotions books address big feelings as a management challenge — "here's how to calm down," "here's what to do when you're angry." A smaller number validate feelings ("it's okay to feel sad"). Almost none directly challenge the shame narrative — "your feelings are NOT too big, your brain is working correctly, and you are not broken."

The QMN angle is a counter-narrative: instead of teaching children to shrink their emotional experience, it teaches them that the feeling makes sense AND that there are things to do with it. This is a different emotional contract from most SEL books.

**Validation required:** Elise must check whether any existing book already owns the "your feelings are not too big / you are not broken" narrative specifically, or whether the market has only generic "it's okay to feel feelings" titles. The distinction matters for differentiation.
