## Market Gap Hypothesis

*Pre-filled for Elise to validate or reject through research.*

**Gap identified:** Most children's anger or big feelings books start at the emotional label. Almost none start at the body — the tight chest, hot face, clenched fists, stomach clench that precede the emotional explosion. This somatic-first framing is widely used in adult therapy and OT contexts but appears underrepresented in genuine children's picture books.

The "storm" metaphor is accessible to children (body feels like weather building) without being clinical. It also naturally illustrates visually: a child's body with storm imagery building inside is a compelling, original illustration concept.

**Validation required:** Elise must confirm that no strong storybook already owns "body signals before anger" framing. If OT workbooks dominate but no storybook exists — gap is real. If a parent-friendly storybook already owns this angle — assess differentiation on age range or metaphor.
