## Market Gap Hypothesis

*Pre-filled for Elise to validate or reject through research.*

**Hypothesis:** Most books in the confidence/courage niche tell children to "just be brave" without acknowledging that the fear itself is real and intelligent. An opportunity exists for a book that:
1. Names and validates the fear feeling (not dismisses it)
2. Reframes the brain's protective instinct as useful, not an enemy
3. Shows courage as one small step — not a transformation

**This is the QMN/MindQuest emotional intelligence angle.** Validate through review mining: do parents mention wishing existing books "acknowledged the fear more" or "didn't just say be brave"?
