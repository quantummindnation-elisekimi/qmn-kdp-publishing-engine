## Copyright / IP Risk Notes

**⚠ FLAG: Inside Out (Disney/Pixar) — MEDIUM risk — requires careful art direction**

Inside Out (2015) and Inside Out 2 (2024) both feature emotions as characters living inside a child's head in a "brain control room." This is the primary IP risk for any children's book that personifies emotions or the brain.

**How to steer clear:**
- The QMN story must be told from the child's perspective — the child IS the protagonist, not personified brain characters
- Do not use a "control room" or "headquarters" visual metaphor
- Do not personify emotions as separate characters living inside the brain
- Do not use Inside Out's color-coding system (Joy = yellow, Anger = red, Sadness = blue, etc.)
- The "loud brain" framing treats the brain as a sensation the child experiences, not a character or setting — this is a meaningful distinction from Inside Out

**Other IP notes:**
- The Awkward Yeti's "Heart and Brain" comic series: adult-only audience, no children's book trademark exposure. The concept of a cartoon brain character exists in adult humor — QMN must ensure any brain-related visual character is clearly original and in a children's picture book format distinctly different from the Awkward Yeti style.
- "Loud Brain" as a character name or title phrase: no existing trademark found. Elise must conduct a USPTO search before finalizing any character name.
- "The Little Loud Brain" as a title: no existing book with this exact title found in Amazon search. Title should still be run through a trademark search before finalizing.
