## Review Mining Notes

*Reorganized from Elise's intake notes. Elise's own words — no review text copied.*

**What parents praise most:**
A simple, repeatable calming tool combined with a story the child engages with emotionally — not just a lecture or worksheet. Parents want something that works in the moment AND is re-readable.

**What parents wish was different:**
Workbooks skew too old and feel clinical rather than like a real story. Parents of children ages 3–6 feel underserved by the activity-book format; they want something that functions as a genuine bedtime or couch read.

**Language parents use to describe the problem:**
Meltdowns, hitting, screaming, "calm down doesn't work," big feelings, mad.

**Unmet needs mentioned in reviews:**
A story-driven picture book for ages 4–7 that a child wants re-read, not a behavior-management worksheet. An upstream angle — reaching the child at the moment of overwhelm BEFORE full anger — appears underserved in the storybook format.

## Market Gap Hypothesis

*Synthesized from Elise's intake gap hypothesis and competitor pattern.*

**Gap identified:** The anger management niche splits cleanly into two lanes:
1. **Workbooks (ages 6–10):** Dominated by therapist-authored activity books with thousands of reviews. Very hard to compete with directly. Ages 6+ skew.
2. **Storybooks (ages 3–6):** One very strong branded series (#7,720, 4,584 reviews) and an animal-metaphor series with ~5,000 reviews. Both own their metaphors (anger identity / dragon).

**The real gap:** Neither lane has a storybook-first book for ages 4–7 that enters through the overwhelm-before-anger doorway — the moment when the brain gets "loud" before the meltdown arrives. This upstream angle treats anger as information the brain is signaling, rather than a behavior problem to suppress. No dragon, no spot, no blob. Brain-first, overwhelm-first, storybook-first.

**Validation still required:**
- Confirm "loud brain children's book" and "calm brain children's book" return no direct match with 200+ reviews
- Confirm the overwhelming majority of existing storybook entries still rely on the anger-management framing (not the overwhelm/upstream framing)
- Validate whether any 2025–2026 titles have entered with this exact angle

## Copyright / IP Risk Notes

*Preliminary from intake and existing scorecard 004 — Elise confirms during research.*

⚠️ **INSIDE OUT (Pixar/Disney) — PRIMARY IP FLAG**
Inside Out is a registered Disney/Pixar property featuring anthropomorphized emotions living inside a character's head, including a character named "Anger." Any QMN brain character must not:
- Resemble the Inside Out visual world (colorful personified emotion characters, control-room imagery)
- Use emotion-color coding similar to Inside Out (red = anger, etc.)
- Feature characters living inside a head in a way that references the Inside Out concept

**How to steer clear:** The QMN "loud brain" approach centers the brain itself as a sensory/neurological experience — not personified emotions as characters. The child IS the brain. No emotion-people inside a head. This distinction must be maintained through all illustration briefs.

**Additional IP risks:**
- "Grumpy Monkey" — trademarked; do not use monkey characters in an anger context
- Train Your Angry Dragon — dragon metaphor in anger context is owned; do not use
- A Little SPOT — spot/blob metaphor is owned; do not use
- Any specific character name must be searched on USPTO.gov before finalizing

**Overall IP risk level:** [ ] Low  [ ] **Medium-High** ✓ *(Inside Out proximity is the primary structural risk — manageable with careful character design, but cannot be treated casually)*

**Disqualifying?** No — score is not ≤ 3 if executed carefully. But character design requires deliberate IP navigation before any illustration work begins.
