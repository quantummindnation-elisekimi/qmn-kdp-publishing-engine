## 3. Why the "Loud Brain" Title Lane Was Rejected

Elise's manual Amazon research found the "loud / brain / too loud" title space to be crowded and carrying positioning risks. Observed close title/result patterns:

- *When Things Get Too Loud*
- *When My Brain Feels Loud*
- *My Brain Is Loud*
- *My Mind Is Too Loud*
- *When My Thoughts Get Loud*
- *When My World Feels Too Loud*
- *My Brain Is a Race Car*
- *My Brain Is Messy*
- *My Wiggly Brain*
- *Eric's Noisy Brain*
- *Too Loud, Too Bright, Too Fast, Too Tight*
- *My Brain Won't Sit Still*
- *The Little Brain People*

**Rejection reasons:**

| Reason | Detail |
|---|---|
| Crowded title space | "Loud," "noisy," "messy," "wiggly," and "busy" brain titles already occupy the phrase space |
| Wrong positioning lane | The existing "loud brain" books cluster around sensory overload, autism, ADHD, and neurodivergence — QMN's general-overwhelm angle would appear to be a me-too entry in that lane |
| Weaker originality | Arriving as "When My Brain Gets Loud" after multiple existing books use near-identical phrasing reduces Amazon discoverability and brand distinctiveness |
| Series risk | If "Little Loud Brain" is used as the Amazon-facing title after this research, it requires significantly stronger differentiation in cover design and description to avoid visual confusion with adjacent titles |

**Internal use only:** "Loud brain" and "The Little Loud Brain" remain valid as internal working titles for QMN's conceptual architecture. Do not use as final Amazon title, subtitle, or keyword field.

## 4. Updated Title Strategy

**Move away from:** Brain-first / loud-brain wording
**Move toward:** Big feelings + pre-burst moment + calm-down resolution

**Core positioning to preserve in the title:**
- The book is about the moment *before* the explosion — the overwhelm signal, not the meltdown itself
- It names what the child feels (big feelings, overwhelm) and where it's headed (burst, anger)
- It promises something (learning to calm down / what to do before anger takes over)

**Keyword priorities for Amazon-facing title and subtitle:**
- `big feelings` — high-search, well-understood parent term
- `overwhelm` — used by parents searching for regulation tools
- `anger` — the highest-demand keyword in the niche (must appear in subtitle if not title)
- `calm down` — high parent search intent
- `emotional regulation` — key SEL term, drives school/library buyer intent
- `storybook` or `story` — signals format, filters out workbook comparisons
- `ages 4–7` or `kids` — age specificity aids discoverability

**Format principle:** Title should be child-readable and emotionally resonant. Subtitle carries the keyword load for Amazon search. Title does not need to stuff keywords if the subtitle is strong.

## 9. Decision Rule

### Green Light (proceed to Gate 8 sign-off)

- No exact-title match in Amazon Books with meaningful review count (100+ reviews)
- No exact-phrase Google result showing a widely-distributed children's product with this title
- No live or pending USPTO trademark conflict
- Amazon broad search confirms active parent demand for big feelings / overwhelm / anger / calm-down keywords

### Caution (flag to Elise before proceeding)

- A similar (not exact) title exists in a different audience or age category
- A low-traction self-published title (under 50 reviews) uses very similar wording — note and assess whether it creates consumer confusion risk
- USPTO shows a dead/cancelled mark — note and proceed with awareness

### Reject (do not use this title)

- An exact or near-exact children's book title exists with strong reviews (100+ reviews)
- A dominant competitor in the niche (500+ reviews) uses very similar wording
- A live USPTO trademark conflict appears on the exact phrase or a primary component of it
