## 3. Why the "Loud Brain" Title Lane Was Rejected

Elise's manual Amazon research found the "loud / brain / too loud" title space to be crowded and positioned in the wrong lane. Observed close title/result patterns:

- *When Things Get Too Loud*
- *When My Brain Feels Loud*
- *My Brain Is Loud*
- *My Mind Is Too Loud*
- *When My Thoughts Get Loud*
- *When My World Feels Too Loud*
- *My Brain Is a Race Car*
- *My Brain Is Messy*
- *My Wiggly Brain*
- *Eric's Noisy Brain*
- *Too Loud, Too Bright, Too Fast, Too Tight*
- *My Brain Won't Sit Still*
- *The Little Brain People*

**Rejection reasons:**

| Reason | Detail |
|---|---|
| Crowded title space | "Loud," "noisy," "messy," "wiggly," and "busy" brain titles already occupy the phrase space |
| Wrong positioning lane | Existing "loud brain" books cluster around sensory overload, autism, ADHD, and neurodivergence — QMN's general-overwhelm angle would appear as a me-too entry in that lane |
| Weaker originality | Arriving as "When My Brain Gets Loud" after multiple near-identical titles reduces Amazon discoverability and brand distinctiveness |
| USPTO proximity | USPTO search showed nearby active Little Brain-related marks — see Section 10A |

**Internal use only:** "Loud brain" and "The Little Loud Brain" remain valid as internal QMN working-concept language. Do not appear in any Amazon-facing title, subtitle, keyword field, series name, or cover.

### Standing Decision Rules (for future title validation)

**Green Light:** No exact-title match with 100+ reviews; no Google collision; no live USPTO conflict; adjacent demand confirmed.

**Caution:** Similar (not exact) title in different audience; low-traction self-published title with very similar wording; dead/cancelled USPTO mark.

**Reject:** Exact or near-exact children's book title with 100+ reviews; dominant competitor (500+ reviews) with very similar wording; live USPTO trademark conflict.
