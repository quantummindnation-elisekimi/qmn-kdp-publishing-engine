## 2. Visual Objective

This character sheet must lock the caring adult's consistent visual identity before any spread image involving the adult is generated.

The caring adult appears across four consecutive spreads — pages 19–26 (spreads 8–11). She is the emotional anchor of the regulation sequence: she arrives, she sits with Noa, she names what Noa is feeling, she stays. Her presence is the quiet turning point of the book.

**What this character sheet must establish and lock:**

- **Same face** — the caring adult must be recognisable as the same woman across all four spreads; her face, expression, and warmth must be consistent throughout
- **Same hairstyle** — soft warm-brown, modern everyday style; soft waves or softly tied back; polished but natural
- **Same skin tone** — warm mid-tone; close and complementary to Noa's warm mid-tone
- **Same outfit** — soft pastel yellow plain midi dress; same dress, same colour across all appearances
- **Same proportions** — natural adult proportions; no cartoon exaggeration
- **Same emotional register** — calm, warm, and unhurried in every panel; never alarmed, never lecturing, never fixing
- **Same body language language rule** — always at Noa's level in key moments; never looming, never visually dominating

**Her visual role:** She is a calm anchor, not a hero. She does not solve the problem. She does not fix the card. She does not tell Noa what to feel. She simply arrives, sees Noa, and stays — and that is enough.

The approved character sheet becomes the master visual reference for all spread generation steps involving the caring adult.

## 4. Art Style Direction

**Match the approved Noa master reference exactly.** The caring adult must be illustrated in the same visual language as Noa — same flatness, same handmade quality, same matte texture.

### Use — Primary Style (must match Noa)

- Flat handmade children's picture-book illustration — paper-cut or gouache-inspired
- Simple rounded forms — adult body defined by colour shapes, not outline weight
- Minimal facial features — simple eyes, soft cheeks, gentle expression
- Matte, non-glossy finish — no shine, no rendering, no gradient fills
- Warm muted colour palette — earthy skin tones, flat colour areas with subtle surface texture
- Cozy, emotionally honest, quiet picture-book quality
- Natural adult proportions — simplified and rounded, not glamorised or exaggerated
- Body language carries emotional register; face is readable and calm
- Neutral white or very light warm paper tone background for all panels

### Avoid — Style

- Disney-style character design
- Pixar-style character design
- DreamWorks-style character design
- Nickelodeon-style character design
- Sesame Street-style character design
- Studio Ghibli-style character design
- Anime or manga style
- 3D render or CGI appearance
- Glossy or cinematic rendering
- Hyper-realistic or photographic rendering
- Thick cartoon outlines or hard vector edges
- Fashion illustration aesthetic

### Avoid — Role and Expression

- Stern parent expression
- Angry parent expression
- Worried or panicked expression
- Lecturing pose (pointing finger, direct instruction gesture)
- Looming over a child
- Fixing or reaching-to-correct gesture
- Hands on hips (authoritative stance)
- Teacher-at-a-whiteboard posture
- Therapist-in-a-therapy-office aesthetic
- Glamorous or aspirational appearance

### Avoid — IP and Safety

- Any visual resemblance to A Little SPOT characters
- Any visual resemblance to Inside Out / Pixar characters
- Any resemblance to any known franchise's parent or caregiver character
- Emotion creatures of any kind — the adult is a real person, not a metaphor
- Brain characters or control-room imagery
- Named artist style references of any kind

## 5. Character Sheet Layout — 6 Panels

The character sheet is a single image containing **exactly 6 panels — no more, no less.** All panels show the same caring adult woman. Outfit, hairstyle, and face must remain visually consistent across all 6 panels. Pose and body position change per panel; everything else remains locked.

**Consistency requirement across all 6 panels:**
- Same face shape in every panel
- Same hair length and hair shape in every panel
- Same skin tone in every panel
- Same outfit and outfit colours in every panel
- Same age and proportions in every panel

**Important:** The adult must not overpower the sheet. She is a supporting character. Her posture in every panel should communicate *I am here with you* — not *I am the solution.* Her body language is always open, low, and calm.

| Panel | View / Pose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Panel 1** | Full body, front view, neutral calm expression | Establishes the caring adult's full character design — the master reference panel; standing or lightly resting, relaxed posture |
| **Panel 2** | Full body, three-quarter view, gentle smile | Natural warm smile — not a performance, not a reassurance-display; the smile of a parent who is genuinely unhurried |
| **Panel 3** | Full body, side profile, calm standing posture | Shows silhouette, hair profile, and clothing from the side — must clearly match the front-view adult |
| **Panel 4** | Full body, seated or crouched at child's level — at the level of a 5–7 year old, not an adult height | Open, relaxed posture; no furniture needed — floor-level crouch or low seat is enough; body open and non-looming |
| **Panel 5** | Upper body, sitting beside an implied child's position, listening quietly — not touching, not fixing, simply present | The adult's face is turned gently toward the implied child; expression is attentive and still; hands resting naturally, not reaching |
| **Panel 6** | Upper body, gentle encouraging expression — soft eyes, slight forward lean, one hand resting naturally (on lap or surface, not reaching toward child) | The quiet "I see you" moment; the face is warm but not dramatically expressive; body is calm and close without crowding |

> **Key instruction to generation tool:** Generate exactly 6 panels — not 7, not 5, exactly 6. Arrange in a 3×2 or 2×3 grid. All 6 panels must show the same adult woman. Reject any sheet where the adult's face, hair, skin tone, or proportions differ between panels.

Using Noa's approved master reference as Image Guidance is the most reliable way to achieve a style match. Text prompts alone cannot fully communicate the flat paper-cut quality of Noa's approved style.

**Steps in Leonardo:**
1. Open the generation panel and locate **Image Guidance** (also called Image to Image or Style Reference depending on Leonardo version)
