## 2. Visual Objective

This character sheet must lock Noa's consistent visual identity before a single spread image is generated.

AI image generation cannot reliably produce the same child across 13 different spread images without an approved reference. If Noa's face, hair, skin tone, outfit, or proportions shift between spreads, the book cannot be assembled as a coherent publication.

**What this character sheet must establish and lock:**

- **Same face** — Noa's face must be recognisable as the same child in every panel of the sheet, and then carried consistently into every spread
- **Same hairstyle** — soft warm-brown, slightly shaggy medium length, natural and unstyled
- **Same skin tone** — warm mid-tone
- **Same outfit** — plain soft teal short-sleeve top, warm cream knee-length shorts, simple cream indoor shoes; neat and presentable throughout; no school-uniform or formal collar look
- **Same proportions** — natural child proportions with gentle picture-book softness; no mascot-like or exaggerated cartoon proportions
- **Same emotional range** — Noa's face and body must convey the full arc from joy to overwhelm to calm without losing character identity
- **Same art style** — flat handmade picture-book illustration; gouache / paper-cut inspired; matte and cozy throughout

The approved character sheet becomes the master visual reference for all subsequent generation steps.

## 4. Art Style Direction

### Use — Primary Style Direction (Elise confirmed)

- Flat handmade children's picture-book illustration — paper-cut or gouache-inspired
- Simple rounded forms — child's body defined by colour shapes, not outline weight
- Minimal facial features — tiny simple eyes (curved marks, not large circles), small soft rosy cheeks, gentle expression
- Matte, non-glossy finish — no shine, no rendering, no gradient fills
- Warm muted colour palette — earthy skin tones, flat colour areas with subtle surface texture
- Cozy, emotionally honest, quiet picture-book quality
- Natural child proportions — simplified and rounded, but not mascot-like or exaggerated
- Body language and posture carry emotion; face is simple and readable, not overly expressive or animated
- Neutral white or very light warm paper tone background for all panels

### Avoid — Style

- Disney-style character design
- Pixar-style character design
- DreamWorks-style character design
- Nickelodeon-style character design
- Sesame Street-style character design
- Studio Ghibli-style character design
- Anime or manga style
- 3D render or CGI appearance
- Hyper-realistic or photographic rendering
- Thick cartoon outlines or hard vector edges

### Avoid — IP and Safety

- Any visual resemblance to A Little SPOT characters
- Any visual resemblance to Inside Out / Pixar emotion characters
- Any resemblance to Train Your Angry Dragon
- Any mascot-character design aesthetic
- Emotion creatures of any kind
- Blob or spot figures
- Brain characters or brain-control-room imagery
- Magical aura, glow, or superpower effects
- Named artist style references of any kind

### Secondary Style Option — Soft Painted Realism

Use only if the flat handmade direction cannot be achieved in 2–3 generation rounds. This is not the preferred direction.

- Warm modern children's picture-book illustration
- Soft emotional realism — not cartoony-flat, not hyper-detailed
- Gentle painterly texture — gouache, watercolour, or high-quality digital painterly media
- Cozy, tender, emotionally honest quality
- Eyes small and natural — realistic child eye size, not doll-like
- Soft gentle linework — avoid thick cartoon outlines

**Note:** Elise's confirmed preference is the flat handmade direction above. Use soft painted realism only as a fallback if flat handmade generation fails repeatedly.

## 5. Character Sheet Layout — 8 Panels

The character sheet is a single image containing **exactly 8 panels — no more, no less.** Do not generate 9 panels. Do not add an extra panel. All panels show the same Noa. Outfit and hairstyle must remain visually consistent across all 8 panels. Expressions and poses change per panel; everything else remains locked.

**Consistency requirement across all 8 panels:**
- Same face shape in every panel
- Same hair length and hair shape in every panel
- Same skin tone in every panel
- Same outfit and outfit colours in every panel
- Same age and proportions in every panel
- Side profile (Panel 3) must clearly match the front-view child (Panel 1)

| Panel | View / Expression | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Panel 1** | Full body, front view, neutral calm expression | Establishes Noa's full character design — the master reference panel |
| **Panel 2** | Full body, three-quarter view, gentle smile | Natural, warm — relaxed happiness; not exaggerated joy |
| **Panel 3** | Full body, side profile, calm posture | Shows Noa's silhouette and hair profile — must clearly match Panel 1 child |
| **Panel 4** | Full body, happy / excited — barely-contained enthusiasm | Open arms or leaning forward eagerly; the opening joy of spread 1 |
| **Panel 5** | Upper body, small frustration — slight brow crease, pencil grip tightening | The first body signal; not dramatic distress; still trying; reader notices before Noa does |
| **Panel 6** | Full body, overwhelmed / almost-burst — shoulders up near ears, cheeks flushed, hands clenched, chest held full | Maximum felt distress; body contracted with the size of the feeling; right at the edge — not screaming, not a meltdown caricature |
| **Panel 7** | Upper body, hand-on-heart pose — one hand resting on chest, eyes soft and inward | Quiet and real, not dramatic; the regulation moment |
| **Panel 8** | Full body, calm and settled — shoulders down, hands open, face soft | Post-regulation calm; the same child as Panel 1 but with something quietly carried forward |

> **Key instruction to generation tool:** Generate exactly 8 panels — not 9, not 7, exactly 8. Arrange in a 4×2 or 2×4 grid. All 8 panels must show the same child. The side profile must clearly match the front-view child. Reject any sheet with 9 panels or where Noa's face, hair, skin tone, or proportions differ between panels.

## 6. Main Image-Generation Prompt

*Use this prompt with Leonardo.ai. Run as a single prompt to produce one character sheet image containing exactly 8 panels.*

---

**PROMPT:**

Create a character reference sheet for a children's picture book.

**Output format:** Create exactly 8 panels, no more and no less. Do not add a ninth panel. Arrange the 8 panels in a clear 4x2 grid (preferred) or 2x4 grid. Every panel must show the same child character.

**Character:** The child is named Noa. Noa is a boy, age 5–7, with warm mid-tone skin, soft warm-brown slightly shaggy medium-length hair — natural and unstyled — small natural dark eyes (realistic child size, not oversized, not doll-like), and a soft rounded child face, gentle and likable, tidy and cared-for. Natural child proportions with gentle picture-book softness.

**Outfit — same in every panel:** plain soft teal short-sleeve top (optional subtle rounded neckline if generated naturally; avoid school-uniform or overly formal collar look), warm cream knee-length shorts, simple cream indoor shoes. No logos, no patterns, no costume elements. Neat and presentable.

**Consistency rules — all 8 panels must show the exact same Noa:** same face shape, same hair length, same hair shape, same skin tone, same outfit, same outfit colours, same age, same body proportions. The side profile must clearly match the front-view child. Do not let any panel look like a different child.

**Art style:** flat handmade picture-book illustration, paper-cut inspired, gouache-like matte texture, simple rounded forms, minimal facial detail, warm muted colour palette, not glossy, not cinematic, not animation-studio-like. Cozy, emotionally honest, child-friendly. Avoid mascot-like proportions. Background for all panels: neutral white or very light warm paper tone only. No background scene details.

Panel 1: full body, front view, neutral calm expression, arms relaxed at sides.
Panel 2: full body, three-quarter view, gentle natural smile, relaxed posture.
Panel 3: full body, side profile view, calm standing posture, hair clearly visible in profile.
Panel 4: full body, happy and excited expression, leaning forward eagerly, arms slightly spread — barely-contained enthusiasm.
Panel 5: upper body, small frustration — slight brow crease, mouth set in determination, hand gripping a pencil slightly too firmly — trying to work through something.
Panel 6: full body, overwhelmed / almost-burst — shoulders raised toward ears, cheeks flushed warm, hands tense and clenched, chest held full and tight, whole body contracted with the size of the feeling — right at the edge, about to overflow, but not yet screaming.
Panel 7: upper body, one hand resting gently on chest over heart, eyes soft and turned slightly inward — quiet and real, not dramatic.
Panel 8: full body, calm and settled — shoulders relaxed and down, hands open and resting loosely, face soft and steady.

**Final constraints:** Exactly 8 panels total. All panels show the same Noa. No text, no labels, no captions, no watermarks.
