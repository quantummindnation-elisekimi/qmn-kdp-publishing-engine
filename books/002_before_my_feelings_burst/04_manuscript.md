**For Grown-Ups**

---

**What "Before Feelings Burst" Means**

Feelings don't arrive at full strength all at once — they build first. Our bodies always send a signal before the burst: tight hands, a warm tummy, a breath that quickens. When a child learns to notice these signals early, there is a moment to pause. This book gives you and your child a shared language for that moment.

---

**Early Body Signals — Does Your Child Notice These?**

Ask your child: *"Where do YOU feel your feelings before they get big?"*

- [ ] Hands squeezing tight
- [ ] Tummy feeling warm or heavy
- [ ] Breathing getting faster
- [ ] Voice getting louder without meaning to
- [ ] Face feeling hot
- [ ] Shoulders going up toward the ears

*"My body gives me clues."*

---

**3 Phrases to Try in the Moment**

1. *"I think something is building in your body — can you feel it?"*
2. *"Let's try: hand on heart, one breath. Can we name the feeling?"*
3. *"Your feelings make sense. Let's name it before it bursts."*

**Tip:** Practice these during calm moments first — so they're familiar before a big-feelings episode arrives.

---

**My Feelings Signal Map** *(for your child)*

Draw yourself.
Mark where you feel your feelings building in your body.
What does each feeling feel like? *(tight, warm, fluttery, heavy, buzzy)*
