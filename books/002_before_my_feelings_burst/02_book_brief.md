## 2. Core Positioning

**The upstream angle:** Most children's books about anger and big feelings enter at the peak — the meltdown, the yell, the burst. *Before My Feelings Burst* enters earlier, at the moment feelings are building but have not yet overflowed. This is the moment parents can intervene. This is the moment a child can learn to notice.

**The parent promise:** *Before My Feelings Burst* gives families a shared language for the feeling that builds before the burst — before the shouting, before the tears, before the shutdown. Parents who read this book with their child gain an opening to say "Are your feelings building right now?" before the meltdown arrives.

**The child promise:** A child reading this book learns that their body always sends a signal first. Feelings don't just arrive; they build. And when you notice the building, you have a moment to pause.

**Positioning statement (internal):**
*Before My Feelings Burst* is the only children's picture storybook for ages 4–7 that enters the overwhelm story at the build-up moment — before the peak — teaching children to recognise body signals and use one simple tool before their feelings overflow.

**What this book is NOT:**
- Not a workbook or activity-book format
- Not an anger-suppression book ("stop feeling angry")
- Not a shame-based correction book ("your anger is wrong")
- Not a sensory/neurodivergence-specific book
- Not a therapy replacement

## 3. Story Concept

### Selected Concept: The Body Signals Story

**Concept title (internal working reference):** *The Body Signals Story*

**Concept summary:**

A child has an ordinary morning that quietly starts to go wrong. Nothing dramatic — something small. A piece won't fit where it should. Something spills. A sibling's presence at the wrong moment. Small. But the feeling that enters is not small.

The child tries to push past it. It's fine. It's fine. But the body knows before the mind does. Hands squeeze tighter. Tummy feels warm. Breath gets faster. Voice gets louder. The feeling is building — and it is about to burst.

At the moment before the burst, someone helps the child notice what their body has already been saying. The child places a hand on their heart, takes one slow breath, and names what they feel out loud. The feeling doesn't go away — but it doesn't burst. The child reconnects, repairs, and carries one new thing forward: feelings always send a signal first.

**What makes this concept original:**

The story's vehicle is not a metaphor-character (no dragon, no blob, no brain-figure). It is the child's own body sensation experience — told from the inside. The physical build-up is made visible through story events (the child squeezing their pencil, their voice going up, their face going hot) rather than through an external creature or object. This is consistent with somatic emotional literacy approaches used by school counselors and occupational therapists, and it is genuinely absent from the current picture book landscape.

**Concept guardrails — all confirmed:**
- Child is protagonist at all times ✅
- No separate emotion characters ✅
- No brain-character or brain-as-setting ✅
- No colored emotion figures ✅
- No external metaphor-creature (no dragon, no monster) ✅
- No blob/spot visual system ✅
- Emotion expressed through body sensations, story events, and environment ✅

## 5. Unique Angle

**Differentiator 1 — Upstream entry point**
Every major competitor in the anger/big feelings storybook category enters the story at or after the emotional peak. *I Am Stronger Than Anger* (4,584 reviews) begins at anger. *Train Your Angry Dragon* (~5,000 reviews) is about taming an already-raging force. *Before My Feelings Burst* enters before any of them — at the overwhelm build-up. This is a genuinely unoccupied story position.

**Differentiator 2 — Body-sensation storytelling, not metaphor-object storytelling**
The dominant picture book approach assigns the emotion to an external creature (dragon, spot, volcano). *Before My Feelings Burst* tells the emotion story through the child's own physical experience — tight hands, warm tummy, fast breath, loud voice. This reflects how therapists and OTs actually teach emotional awareness, and it is absent from the current top-selling picture books. No other title in the confirmed Amazon search results uses this approach.

**Differentiator 3 — One simple tool, not a curriculum**
Anger/big feelings workbooks and some storybooks offer 5–10 strategies. This book teaches exactly one: name it before it bursts (hand on heart + one breath + ask for a pause). One tool, taught through a single story, that a child can remember and use. Re-readability comes from the story, not from rotating through activities.

**Differentiator 4 — Non-shaming emotional framing**
The book's emotional contract is: your feelings are not wrong. They just need to be noticed before they overflow. This is distinct from anger-management books that (even unintentionally) frame the emotion as a problem to fix rather than a signal to understand.

**Why a parent would choose this over competitors:**
A parent who has already tried "calm down" commands and found them ineffective needs something that meets the child earlier — before the escalation. The title alone (*Before My Feelings Burst*) signals a different intervention point. The body-signal framing gives parents language to use before the meltdown, not only during or after it.

## 6. Emotional Arc

| Stage | Child's Emotional Experience |
|---|---|
| **Opening** | Child is happy, in their world — something enjoyable is happening |
| **First friction** | A small frustration enters — ordinary, not dramatic |
| **Suppression** | Child tries to push through: "It's fine. It's fine." Body is already responding |
| **Body signals building** | Hands tighten. Tummy warms. Breath quickens. Voice rises slightly. Child does not yet notice |
| **Second friction** | One more small thing goes wrong — feeling stacks on the first |
| **Pre-burst moment** | Feeling is almost too big. The burst is coming. Child and reader can both feel it |
| **Turning point** | A caring adult (or the child's own flicker of self-awareness) catches the early signal: "I can feel something building" |
| **Regulation moment** | Child places hand on heart. Breathes once slowly. Names the feeling out loud: "I feel [overwhelmed / too full / like I might burst]" |
| **The feeling settles** | Not disappears — settles. The burst does not happen. Child and adult sit together briefly |
| **Reconnection** | Child returns to the moment — the frustration is still real, but it is no longer in control |
| **Closing takeaway** | Child (and reader) understands: feelings always send a signal first. Now I know what to listen for |

**Takeaway for the child reader:**
*My feelings don't just burst — they build first. And when I notice them building, I can take one breath and name them before they overflow.*

## 7. Regulation Tool

**Primary tool: Name It Before It Bursts**

**Three-step sequence (one integrated tool, not three separate tools):**

1. **Hand on heart** — places physical attention on the body, breaks the automatic escalation loop
2. **One slow breath** — the breath is the pause; it does not need to be counted or elaborate
3. **Name it out loud** — "I feel _____ and it's getting bigger" — naming the feeling before it peaks

**Why one tool only:**
Children ages 4–7 cannot retain and retrieve a multi-step toolkit during emotional escalation. One sequence, embedded in a memorable story moment, can be recalled and used. The goal is that after reading this book multiple times, the child and parent share a common signal: "Hand on heart?"

**What this tool does NOT claim:**
- It does not eliminate big feelings
- It does not guarantee the child will stop a meltdown every time
- It is one skill in an ongoing emotional learning process
- It should be practiced during calm moments, not only during escalation

**Back matter note for parents:** The tool is described explicitly in the parent section at the back of the book, with suggestions for practicing it during calm moments before it is needed in a crisis moment.

## 9. Parent / Teacher Back Matter Plan

*Page content direction only — no final copy. One 32-page spread.*

### "What 'Before Feelings Burst' Means" (parent note — approx. 60–80 words)

A short paragraph explaining the book's core idea: feelings don't arrive at full intensity instantly — they build first, and bodies send early signals. When a child (or adult) notices those signals early, there is a moment to pause before the feeling overflows. This book gives children and caregivers a shared language for that moment.

### Early Body Signals Checklist

*A simple visual checklist — not clinical, not diagnostic.*

- [ ] Hands squeezing tight
- [ ] Tummy feeling warm or fluttery
- [ ] Breathing getting faster
- [ ] Voice getting louder without meaning to
- [ ] Face feeling hot
- [ ] Body wanting to move or run

*Instruction: "Ask your child: where do YOU feel your feelings before they get big?"*

### 3 Parent Phrases to Use in the Moment

1. "Can you feel something building in your body right now?"
2. "Let's try — hand on heart, one breath. What does the feeling want to say?"
3. "Your feelings make sense. Let's name it before it bursts."

*Note: Use these during calm moments first, so they are familiar before a big-feelings episode arrives.*

### 1 Child Activity: "My Feelings Signal Map"

*One simple activity — no worksheet needed; can be done with any piece of paper.*

> Draw yourself. Mark where you feel your feelings building in your body. What does each feeling feel like? (tight, warm, fluttery, heavy, buzzy)

## 10. QMN / MindQuest Brand Fit

**Core connection:**
The QMN / MindQuest brand is built on the idea that understanding what is happening inside you — before it controls you — is a learnable skill. *Before My Feelings Burst* is the clearest expression of that idea for young children. It is not about suppressing feelings; it is about understanding them early enough to respond rather than react.

**Research-backed foundation:**
The body-signal approach in this book reflects established somatic emotional literacy principles used by school counselors, occupational therapists, and child psychologists. The book doesn't cite research — it tells a story that embodies it. Parents who are familiar with regulation-focused approaches will recognize the framework immediately.

**Parent-useful:**
The back matter gives parents three specific phrases and one activity. The book functions as both a story children return to and a tool parents can reference during real-life moments.

**Emotionally safe:**
The book does not shame the child. It does not say "don't feel that." It says: your feelings always send a signal first, and now you know how to listen. This is a positive, strengths-based emotional framing.

**Original:**
No existing children's picture book identified in Gate 7 or Gate 8 research uses the upstream-entry + body-sensation approach for the general overwhelm-before-anger audience (ages 4–7). The nearest competitors are either workbook-format, peak-anger-focused, or targeted at sensory/neurodivergence audiences.

**Series format without Little Brain branding:**
*Before My Feelings Burst* stands alone without requiring any "Little Brain" series branding. It can be cross-sold with other QMN books through shared aesthetic and brand voice rather than through a series character name. If a series brand is developed later, it can grow from the QMN / MindQuest brand identity rather than from the rejected "Little Brain" phrase. This removes the USPTO/trademark risk while preserving the compounding cross-sell structure.
