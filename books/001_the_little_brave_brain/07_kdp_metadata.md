Does your child freeze when something new comes along?

Maybe it's a new classroom. A new activity. A new food. The tears come before they even try. And no matter how much you say "just give it a chance," the fear holds tight.

*The Little Brave Brain* is a gentle picture book for children ages 4–7 who feel scared to try new things — and for the parents who love them and want to help without pushing.

Wren knows what it feels like when the brain starts talking. *What if it's bad? What if something goes wrong?* But when Wren watches a friend take one small step, something shifts. Maybe brave doesn't mean the scared feeling goes away. Maybe brave means trying anyway — in the smallest possible step.

This warm, honest story validates what your child feels while gently showing that courage is a muscle that grows one small try at a time. A perfect read-aloud for anxious or cautious children, bedtime conversations, and the first day of anything new.

*Part of the MindQuest Little Brain series by QMN — books that help children understand their feelings and grow from the inside out.*
