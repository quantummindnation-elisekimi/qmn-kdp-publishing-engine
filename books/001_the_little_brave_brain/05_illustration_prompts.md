## Illustration Style Guide

- **Overall visual style:** Soft, rounded, warm. Watercolor-wash feel with gentle line work. Nothing harsh or angular.
- **Color palette:**
  - Warm state (home, safety, after courage): Golden yellows, soft peach, warm terracotta, sage green
  - Transitional state (facing the new thing): Soft cool blues and muted lavender mixed into the warm palette
  - Resolution: Full warm palette return, brighter than the opening
- **Line quality:** Gentle, slightly loose — not rigid. Illustrations should feel handmade and warm.
- **Character rendering:** Rounded shapes for all characters. Large expressive eyes. Soft, approachable forms.
- **Background detail:** Medium — enough to establish setting without overwhelming the character focus.
- **Emotional tone:** Quiet, genuine, intimate. Like reading by lamplight.
- **What to avoid:**
  - No sharp angular character designs
  - No superhero aesthetics
  - No visual elements resembling known animated franchise characters
  - No photorealistic style
  - No cold or clinical color palettes
  - No artist style references of any kind

### Spread 1 (Pages 1–2)

**Scene:** A small child (Wren) sits comfortably in a cozy, familiar room — perhaps on a soft rug, near a bookshelf, or at a low table with a well-loved toy. Everything is warm and known.
**Characters:** Wren, alone, relaxed and content. Expression: peaceful, a small gentle smile. Body language completely open.
**Environment:** Interior home scene. Soft morning light through a curtain. Familiar objects — a worn teddy bear, a stack of picture books, a favorite cup. Nothing new or unfamiliar in sight.
**Mood/lighting:** Warm golden morning light. Cozy. Safe.
**Focus:** Wren's comfortable, relaxed expression and posture.
**Composition:** Wren is centered or slightly left; text sits in lower right area.

---

### Spread 2 (Pages 3–4)

**Scene:** Something new appears — glimpsed through a doorway, a window, or introduced by a warm adult figure in the background. Wren's expression shifts from relaxed to uncertain — not scared yet, just... noticing.
**Characters:** Wren in the foreground, expression changing. A gentle adult figure (parent/caregiver) visible at the edge, warm and calm. The new thing is visible but not looming.
**Environment:** The same cozy home, but a doorway or window now frames something unfamiliar beyond it.
**Mood/lighting:** Still warm, but a thin ribbon of cool blue beginning to mix in around Wren's edges.
**Focus:** The contrast between Wren's changing expression and the safe familiar room.
**Composition:** Wren takes up most of the frame; the new thing is glimpsed at the edge, not centered.

---

### Spread 3 (Pages 5–6)

**Scene:** Wren stands at a little distance from the new thing, tilting their head. Looking at it. Uncertain.
**Characters:** Wren, body slightly closed (arms near body, feet planted), head tilted, expression: curious but hesitant.
**Environment:** Outside or near the new thing — whatever context Elise approves (backyard, classroom entrance, pool edge, etc.). The new thing is visible but Wren keeps distance.
**Mood/lighting:** The cool blue color is now more visible around Wren — a soft swirl of cool tones near their head and shoulders.
**Focus:** Wren's posture — hesitant but not fleeing.
**Composition:** Wren on one side, the new thing on the other. Empty space between them representing the distance Wren is keeping.

---

### Spread 4 (Pages 7–8)

**Scene:** A gentle metaphorical representation of Wren's brain "talking." This could be shown as soft thought-bubble shapes around Wren's head containing swirling cool-blue shapes, or as small gentle text-cloud shapes with abstract worry-shapes inside. Keep it friendly and metaphorical — not frightening.
**Characters:** Wren, looking inward. Expression: listening to something. Not panicking — just caught in thought.
**Environment:** The background softens — slightly more dreamlike in this spread, not a literal setting change.
**Mood/lighting:** Coolest lighting in the book — soft blues and lavenders around Wren. Still gentle, not dark.
**Focus:** The thought-swirl around Wren's head and Wren's listening expression.
**Composition:** Wren centered; thought-swirls radiate gently outward.

---

### Spread 5 (Pages 9–10)

**Scene:** Bo appears — slightly closer to the new thing than Wren, relaxed and comfortable. Not looking at Wren with pity or pressure — just being there.
**Characters:** Wren watching Bo from a slight distance. Bo is calm, open posture, a small easy smile.
**Environment:** Back to the context of the new thing. Both characters visible together for the first time.
**Mood/lighting:** Bo brings warm color energy back into the scene — where Bo stands is slightly warmer.
**Focus:** The contrast between Wren's uncertainty and Bo's ease.
**Composition:** Wren slightly left and back; Bo slightly right and forward — showing their different positions relative to the new thing.

---

### Spread 6 (Pages 11–12)

**Scene:** Bo takes one small, calm step toward the new thing. Just one step. Simple. Wren watches carefully.
**Characters:** Bo mid-step, easy expression. Wren watching, very slightly leaning forward — the beginning of curiosity overcoming caution.
**Environment:** Same context. The space between Wren and the new thing is slightly smaller now.
**Mood/lighting:** Warm palette creeping back in as something shifts for Wren.
**Focus:** Bo's one small step. Wren's slight lean forward.
**Composition:** Bo in motion, Wren slightly further back but oriented toward Bo and the new thing.

---

### Spread 7 (Pages 13–14)

**Scene:** Wren takes a tiny step of their own. Feet have moved. The fluttery feeling is still shown (small cool swirl) but Wren is moving.
**Characters:** Wren, mid-tiny-step. Expression: concentrated, not happy yet — the effort is visible.
**Environment:** Wren is slightly closer to the new thing than before.
**Mood/lighting:** Split — cool swirl still present but warm light starting to edge in from the direction of the new thing.
**Focus:** Wren's foot taking that one small step.
**Composition:** Low angle emphasizes the step; Wren's face shows effort above.

---

### Spread 8 (Pages 15–16)

**Scene:** Wren pauses mid-approach, looking slightly upward — thinking the thought "brave doesn't mean the scared feeling goes away." Reflective, internal, quiet.
**Characters:** Wren, paused, thoughtful. Expression: something shifting — a small realization.
**Environment:** Quiet background — the world is waiting while Wren thinks.
**Mood/lighting:** Soft, gentle — the cool blue and warm gold are equally balanced in this spread.
**Focus:** Wren's face in the moment of realization.
**Composition:** Wren centered, simpler background than most spreads — this is an internal moment.

---

### Spread 9 (Pages 17–18)

**Scene:** Wren actually tries the new thing. The specific action — the held breath moment. This is the turning point spread.
**Characters:** Wren in action — concentrated, eyes wide, a small wince perhaps, but doing it.
**Environment:** Wren is now at/in/with the new thing.
**Mood/lighting:** This spread holds its breath — the palette is suspended between cool and warm, the moment before resolution.
**Focus:** Wren doing the thing. The action itself.
**Composition:** Full, active composition — Wren takes most of the frame. Dynamic without being aggressive.

---

### Spread 10 (Pages 19–20)

**Scene:** The moment after. Wren is surprised — the thing is okay. Eyes wide in a different way — surprised discovery, not fear.
**Characters:** Wren, expression transforming: the fear-squint becoming eyes-open-wide surprise, the corners of the mouth beginning to lift.
**Environment:** The new thing is visible and it looks... fine. Normal. Not scary.
**Mood/lighting:** Warm palette flooding back in fully. The cool swirl is noticeably smaller or fading.
**Focus:** Wren's face in the moment of surprised relief.
**Composition:** Wren close to the viewer — intimate, emotional. The new thing visible but calm behind them.

---

### Spread 11 (Pages 21–22)

**Scene:** Wren and Bo together, comfortable in the new situation. Both smiling — not dramatically, just settled.
**Characters:** Wren and Bo side by side or nearby, easy body language, a small shared smile.
**Environment:** Within the new thing's setting — it's familiar now.
**Mood/lighting:** Fully warm, bright, resolved.
**Focus:** The connection between Wren and Bo — the shared experience.
**Composition:** Two characters balanced, equal space, comfortable closeness.

---

### Spread 12 (Pages 23–24)

**Scene:** Wren tries again — and again. Multiple small tries shown in one spread, maybe in panels or in a flowing scene.
**Characters:** Wren in motion, each version slightly more confident than the last.
**Environment:** Scenes of the new thing from different angles — showing Wren moving through it.
**Mood/lighting:** Fully warm, energetic, building.
**Focus:** The progression — each step more confident than the last.
**Composition:** Consider a three-part horizontal flow showing Wren's growing confidence.

### Spread 13 (Pages 25–26)

**Scene:** Wren is now fully comfortable in the new thing. They might be showing someone else who just arrived — a new child who looks uncertain.
**Characters:** Wren, open and at ease. Possibly a new uncertain figure that Wren is approaching calmly, like Bo approached Wren earlier.
**Environment:** Wren fully in the new setting, comfortable.
**Mood/lighting:** Warmest spread of the book. Full golden palette.
**Focus:** Wren's transformed body language — fully open, confident, at ease.
**Composition:** Wren centered and upright, the new setting comfortable around them.

### Spread 14 (Pages 27–28)

**Scene:** Wren heading home at day's end, slightly taller and lighter. Warm golden hour light.
**Characters:** Wren walking, small smile, body completely open — no hunched shoulders, no crossed arms.
**Environment:** Outdoor setting, warm sunset. The new thing is now in the background — familiar, behind Wren.
**Mood/lighting:** Golden hour warmth. The coolest colors of the book are entirely gone.
**Focus:** Wren's changed posture and expression compared to the opening.
**Composition:** Wren from behind or three-quarters, walking forward into warm light.

### Spread 15 (Pages 29–30)

**Scene:** Wren and a warm adult figure at home in the evening — cozy, end-of-day, conversation.
**Characters:** Wren and parent/caregiver together. Warm embrace or close sitting. Adult expression: listening, loving, proud.
**Environment:** Home interior, evening light — warm and intimate.
**Mood/lighting:** Intimate warmth. Lamp or soft evening light.
**Focus:** The connection — the adult listening to Wren, not lecturing.
**Composition:** Two figures close, the home around them familiar and safe.

### Spread 16 (Pages 31–32)

**Scene:** Final image — Wren peaceful and settled, perhaps drifting to sleep or quietly reflecting. A small warm golden glow near Wren's head — the "brave brain" idea made visual, gentle and metaphorical.
**Characters:** Wren alone, peaceful, a small content smile.
**Environment:** Wren's bedroom — the safest, most familiar place. But now it feels earned, not just default.
**Mood/lighting:** The softest, warmest light in the book. Quiet. Resolved.
**Focus:** Wren's peaceful expression and the small golden glow.
**Composition:** Quiet, intimate, simple. Wren takes most of the frame; the world is soft around them.
